Script

You know, life has this way of handing you moments / that just feel… unbearable.
Like, when you waste time / you can never get back,
or when you miss an opportunity so important / it haunts you for weeks—maybe even years.

It's those raw moments—the ones you can't undo—that really cut deep.

I mean, think about it.
Missing out on a project / you poured your heart into,
or losing a game / you trained for—
it's not just about the event itself.

It's about the part of yourself / that was tied to it.
It feels like it's not just a loss of time or effort—
it's a loss of identity,
of who you thought you were / or wanted to be.
And yeah, that hurts. A lot.

You know, there's this meme I saw the other day / that sums it up perfectly:
"I don't drink, I don't do drugs. I live with my suffering raw."

It's funny, in a dark, painfully relatable way.
Because that's exactly it, right?

We don't always have a way to numb the pain / or distract ourselves.
Sometimes, you just have to sit with it / as it carves deep scars
into your ego and your sense of self.

But here's the thing.
That pain—it's part of being human.

We all go through it / in one way or another.
It's what it means to live, to care, / to have loves and dislikes.
Nobody escapes unscathed.

And maybe, in a strange way, that's comforting.
It's like, yeah, this hurts,
but it's also what connects us all.

This shared experience of loss, of raw pain, / reminds me that I'm not alone in this.
